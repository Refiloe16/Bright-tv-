# Bright-tv-

**Overview**
BrightTV is a subscription-based streaming service looking to grow its subscriber base for the current financial year. The CEO approached the Customer Value Management (CVM) team to provide data-driven insights that would support this objective. The project involved analysing two datasets — a viewership transactions table and a user profiles table — both housed in Databricks, containing information on 10,000 registered subscribers and their viewing behaviour. The goal was to deliver a 20-minute presentation to the CEO covering user and usage trends, factors that influence consumption, content recommendations for low-traffic days, and initiatives to grow the subscriber base.
**How I Went About It**
The analysis followed a structured five-stage process mapped out in a mind map and process flow diagram. We began with data ingestion, loading the Excel dataset into Databricks and joining the viewership and user profile tables on UserID. A critical preparation step was converting all timestamps from UTC to South African Standard Time using the Africa/Johannesburg timezone. We then cleaned the data by removing ghost sessions under 30 seconds, replacing Python-generated "None" strings with "Unknown" across all demographic fields, and standardising age into six bands ranging from Kids & Youth through to Senior. Once the base dataset was clean and validated, we ran exploratory queries in Databricks SQL covering daily, weekly and monthly trends, hour-of-day and day-of-week patterns, channel popularity, and demographic breakdowns by gender, race, age and province. The results were exported to Excel where pivot tables and charts were built to visualise the findings for the CEO presentation.
**Insights Recovered — Usage Trends**
The data revealed strong and consistent growth in viewership over the analysis period, with sessions nearly doubling from 1,655 in January to 3,576 in March. Afternoon was the peak viewing period with 2,835 sessions, followed by Evening at 2,421 sessions, suggesting subscribers are most active outside of working hours. Friday was the busiest day of the week at 1,246 sessions while Monday was the lowest at 706 sessions, identifying a clear opportunity to drive engagement at the start of the week. Gauteng was by far the most active province with 2,696 sessions, followed by Western Cape at 1,413, indicating strong geographic concentration in the two major economic hubs of South Africa.
**Insights Recovered — Content and Demographics**
Live sport emerged as the single most powerful content driver on the platform. ICC Cricket World Cup 2011 topped the channel rankings with 1,222 sessions and Supersport Live Events followed closely at 1,209, together accounting for nearly a third of all top-channel sessions. This finding directly answers the CEO's question on what content to push on low-traffic days — scheduling live sport events and highlights on Mondays would likely lift the platform's weakest day. On the demographic side, male subscribers dominated consumption at approximately 90% of sessions, while female subscribers recorded only 688 sessions despite being a registered segment of the user base. Black subscribers led by race with 3,198 sessions, followed by Coloured at 1,229 and Indian/Asian at 1,179.
**Growth Recommendations**
The analysis uncovered several clear opportunities to grow BrightTV's subscriber base. A significant pool of registered users had zero recorded viewership sessions, representing an immediate reactivation target that the CVM team can address through personalised push notifications and re-engagement campaigns. The gender imbalance is the largest single growth opportunity — female subscribers are heavily underrepresented and targeted content acquisition and marketing campaigns tailored to female audiences could meaningfully expand the active base. Provinces outside of Gauteng and Western Cape, particularly KwaZulu-Natal, Mpumalanga and Limpopo, show registered users but lower-than-expected activity levels, pointing to geographic expansion potential through localised campaigns. Finally, retaining the top 10% of high-value subscribers through loyalty incentives or exclusive content will protect the revenue base while growth initiatives are executed.
